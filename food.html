Traditional Bidayuh Bau garments are a symbol of their heritage and identity, worn during festivals and ceremonies. Their vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and craftsmanship showcase creativity and skills passed down through generations, fostering pride and cultural understanding.

KAU'

Kau' is a speciality of the Bidayuh Bau people; it is fermented fish. It contains rice powder, coarse sea salt, freshwater fish, and unique plants from the Borneo jungle. The weeks-long fermenting process produces a strong, aromatic meal.

KUBAR

For a sweet treat, sago is transformed into kubar, a type of pancake. Grated coconut and a pinch of salt are added to the sago mixture before frying it in a wok. The result is a crispy and flavorful pancake, perfect for a snack or dessert.

LINUT

This glue-like sago paste can be enjoyed on its own or used as a thickener in soups and stews.
